Group member: Yvette Hupje

1. Project summary

My live report is on the Mala Inventura art festival which takes place annually in various venues across Prague, Czechia. Live, within the context of my project, refers to the live artistic performance events which are not recorded. I attended a performance entitled "Why is She Like This?", based on the true story of Rosemary Kennedy, the sister of US President John F. Kennedy, who was lobotomised as a result of her "uncontrollable" nature.

2. Context and background

  • Mala Inventura is an annual festival of contemporary and experimental theatre
  • It takes place across about 16 venues in Prague
  • The programme mainly consists of premieres, interdisciplinary work, and performance art
  • The events are spread across several days which encourages audiences to move around the city
  • The festival centres emerging and independent artists and is funded by Nová síť and the loca authorities

3. Case studies and inspirations (2–4)

Case study 1: Vice's Cultural Reporting

Link:

Vice's reporting puts the viewer directly inside the event, and uses handheld footage and minimal narrartion to create a sense of immediacy. The filming style allows for a strong sense of the atmosphere and features informal interviews. I found these reportings useful for capturing the "live" feeling of a space.

Screenshot credit + source link.

Case study 2: Arte Track: Short Arts Documentaries

Link: Link to Arte Tracks' YouTube

Arte Tracks makes complex and niche cultural topics and events accessibly in visually engaging way. The handheld camera and shots of travelling to the local area help to contextualise the artists they cover, and I find the combination of this footage and minimal editing and voiceover very engaging and effective in communicating ideas without over-explaining. I attempt to adopt this balance in my live report.

Screenshot credit + source link.

4. Planning and method

  • Format: mixed media (video, photography, ambient audio)
  • Location plan: I initially planned on recording multiple venues across Prague to demonstrate movement across the spaces rather than a single location.
  • Shot plan: I planned for wide shots, showing streets, exterior and interior or the venue(s) and audience flow. Close shots would be any details I find interesting during the performance.
  • Documenting only the events I attended, checking programme details with the festival listings and using original footage as primary evidence.

Planning image 1 (optional)

A map, floorplan, storyboard frame, or location screenshot.

Image credit + source link (if applicable).

Planning image 2 (optional)

A shot list photo, equipment setup, or diagram of camera position.

Image credit + source link (if applicable).

5. Process and drafts

  • What you filmed: I filmed my journey to the venue, capturing the streets of Prague on weekend night. I filmed short clips of the venue and of the performance as I was permitted to film.
  • What is missing / what you still need: I would have liked to get more intervies with the audience, and maybe closer shots of the performance.
  • Edit plan: I plan to edit minimally, with little on screen text for clarity or subtitles if the audio quality is lacking.
  • Ethics and safety notes: I have checked and it is permitted to film the venue and the performance, but I will ensure that I get consent for interviews and avoid disrupting performances or the audience by sitting at the back and ensuring my phone brightness is low. I will remain observational rather than intrusive.
